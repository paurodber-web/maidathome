Our Story

We didn't study cleaning.
We lived it.

When Pau and Stefania landed in Melbourne, they weren't thinking about starting a business. They were thinking about building a life in a new country — and that meant getting to work. Like a lot of new arrivals, cleaning was where they started.

What surprised them wasn't the work itself. It was how much room there was to do it better. Poor communication. Inconsistent results. Cleaners who showed up late, or used whatever products were on hand. Clients who never knew what to expect. The industry wasn't bad — it was just stuck.

The standard we set for ourselves wasn't "good enough for a cleaner." It was: would we be happy coming home to this?

In 2017 they started going directly to clients. No agency, no middleman — just Pau and Stefania showing up consistently, doing exactly what they said they would, and asking for honest feedback. The reputation they built was slow, earned, and real.

In 2024 they formalised everything: the brand, the booking system, the training process. They brought on team members and taught them the same standard. Maid at Home stopped being a two-person operation and became something bigger — without losing the values that made clients trust them in the first place.

Mission & Values

Why consistency
is everything

Most cleaning companies have a great first visit. The problem is the third, the seventh, the fifteenth — when the novelty is gone and it's just another job. That's exactly where Maid at Home is designed not to drop off.

Consistency isn't exciting to talk about, but it's the hardest thing to deliver — and the only thing that actually matters to the people who open their front door at the end of a long day.

A clean home shouldn't depend on who shows up or what kind of day they're having. It should be something you can count on, every single time, without having to check.
— Pau & Stefania, Maid at Home Founders
01

Consistency above everything

The same result on visit one and visit fifty. Every cleaner follows the same structured checklist. No shortcuts, no variation based on who's working that day. Clients should be able to predict exactly what they'll come home to — because they always do.

02

Reliability as a baseline, not a selling point

On time. Prepared. Equipped. Reliable isn't something we boast about — it's the floor, not the ceiling. A cleaner who shows up late or unprepared isn't an inconvenience. It's a broken promise, and we treat it that way.

03

Honest pricing, always

You see the price before we arrive. We don't adjust it on the day. No surprise charges, no fees buried in the booking process. What we quote is what you pay — because trust starts before the first visit.

04

Respect for the people doing the work

Our cleaners are paid fairly, trained properly, and treated like the professionals they are. We believe that how you treat your team is exactly how your team will treat a client's home. There are no shortcuts to that either.

05

Own it when something goes wrong

We don't hide behind small print. If something wasn't cleaned to standard, we fix it — fast, no arguments, no charge. Our 24-hour re-clean guarantee exists because we mean it.

Our Team

Who we let into
your home

Pau and Stefania built Maid at Home on their own reputation. When they started bringing other cleaners on board in 2024, the standard didn't change — it just had to be taught. Every person who cleans for Maid at Home goes through the same process they developed over seven years of doing it themselves.

We don't hire fast to fill a gap. We hire carefully and train thoroughly, because the alternative — a cleaner who doesn't meet the standard — costs us more than an unfilled slot ever would.

🛡️ Every Maid at Home cleaner is:
  • Police-checked before their first assignment
  • Fully insured (public liability and personal injury)
  • Trained on the Maid at Home cleaning checklist
  • Briefed on eco-product use and surface safety
  • Reviewed regularly by clients and by us
1

Application & initial screening

Every applicant goes through a detailed screening before they meet us in person. We look for people with real cleaning experience, strong references, and — just as important — the kind of reliability and care that can't be trained in.

2

Background check

Every cleaner is police-checked before they enter a client's home. No exceptions. Our clients open their doors to our team — that responsibility isn't taken lightly.

3

Hands-on training to our standard

New cleaners are trained on our full checklist — room by room, product by product, surface by surface. Training isn't a walkthrough. It's practical, supervised, and assessed against the same standard we hold ourselves to. If it isn't right, we keep going.

4

Supervised first assignments

The first jobs are reviewed closely — either by Pau or Stefania directly, or via detailed client feedback. We're not hands-off until we're confident the standard is consistent, not just passable.

5

Ongoing reviews & accountability

Client feedback is read by the founders — not passed through a manager or filtered by a system. If a cleaner consistently gets strong feedback, they're recognised for it. If there's a recurring issue, we address it directly. Every cleaner knows the bar is real.

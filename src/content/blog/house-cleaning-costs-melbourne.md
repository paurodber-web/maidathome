## Home size and condition

A larger home normally needs more cleaning time, but condition matters just as much as the number of rooms. A maintained two-bedroom home can require less work than a smaller property with significant buildup.

When requesting a quote, describe the current condition honestly and mention any areas that need special attention. This helps the cleaner recommend a realistic service.

## Hourly and flat-rate cleaning

Hourly cleaning works well when you have a priority list and want the cleaner to focus on selected tasks within an agreed amount of time. Flat-rate services are useful when you want a defined checklist and a clearer total before the booking.

Compare what is included rather than looking only at the headline price. Equipment, cleaning products, insurance and the scope of work can differ between providers.

## Standard and deep cleaning

A Standard Clean is generally intended for homes that receive regular care. A Deep Clean allows more time for detail work and areas with accumulated dust, grease or soap residue.

Choosing the correct service from the beginning reduces the chance of an unrealistic booking or unexpected additions on the day.

## Ask for a clear scope

Before confirming, check which rooms, tasks and extras are included. A transparent quote should make it easy to understand what result the service is designed to deliver.

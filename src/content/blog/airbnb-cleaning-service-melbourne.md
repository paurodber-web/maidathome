## Use the same checklist for every turnover

A repeatable room-by-room checklist reduces missed tasks. It should cover bathrooms, kitchen surfaces, floors, beds, bins and a final presentation check.

Keep property-specific instructions separate from the general cleaning list. This makes access details, linen locations and supply requirements easier for the team to follow.

## Allow time for linen and restocking

Changing beds, handling towels and replenishing guest supplies can add significant time. Confirm whether linen is already laundered and available at the property or whether an additional service is required.

Create minimum stock levels for essentials so shortages can be identified before the next guest arrives.

<div class="article-checklist" role="group" aria-label="Airbnb turnover checklist">
  <strong>Before the next guest arrives</strong>
  <ul>
    <li>Replace linen and towels</li>
    <li>Restock bathroom and kitchen supplies</li>
    <li>Empty bins and check odours</li>
    <li>Test lights, remotes and visible appliances</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Check the property as a guest would

The final walkthrough should include visible surfaces, odours, lighting and the presentation of the entrance. A clean property should also feel ready and welcoming from the moment the guest opens the door.

<blockquote>
<p>Great turnover cleaning is not only about removing dirt. It is about making the next guest feel expected.</p>
<cite>Maid At Home</cite>
</blockquote>

## Communicate issues quickly

Damage, missing items or maintenance problems should be reported as soon as they are noticed. Clear communication gives the host more time to act before check-in and keeps cleaning separate from maintenance decisions.

## Start before the moving day

Whenever possible, arrange access before your belongings arrive. Confirm that utilities are connected and that any painting or repair work has already been completed.

Cleaning after trades have finished prevents fresh dust from settling over surfaces that have just been prepared.

## Work from the inside out

Begin with cupboards, wardrobes and storage areas so they are ready to receive your belongings. Continue with kitchens and bathrooms, then dust the remaining rooms before finishing the floors.

This order helps avoid transferring debris back onto completed areas.

<div class="article-checklist" role="group" aria-label="Move-in cleaning checklist">
  <strong>Before boxes arrive</strong>
  <ul>
    <li>Clean cupboards and wardrobes</li>
    <li>Wipe switches, handles and taps</li>
    <li>Remove dust after repairs</li>
    <li>Finish with vacuuming and mopping</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Focus on high-contact areas

Handles, switches, taps, benches and bathroom fixtures deserve careful attention before the household moves in. Check inside drawers and cupboards as well as the surfaces that are immediately visible.

## Leave floors until last

Vacuuming and mopping should be the final major tasks. Once the floors are complete, limit movement through the property until they are dry and the moving process begins.

A well-planned move-in clean creates a more comfortable starting point and lets you unpack directly into prepared rooms.

What Is It

High-performance cleaning that
revitalises fibers

Carpet fibers act as a magnet for dirt, traffic soot, pet dander, pollen, and dust mites. Standard vacuuming removes surface debris, but leaves deep-rooted contaminants trapped inside the pile.

Our professional carpet service utilizes advanced hot water extraction technology. By infusing high-temperature water (110°C) with eco-friendly cleaning agents, we break down stubborn soils, emulsify grease, and rinse them out with dual-motor vacuum suction.

The result is a deeply sanitized carpet that looks brighter, smells fresher, feels significantly softer, and dries rapidly within hours. Ideal for pet owners, families with babies, and bond returns.

Our Process

No shortcuts. Just deep,
thorough extraction.

We follow a strict multi-step carpet cleaning system to guarantee maximum soil recovery and stain treatment without leaving sticky detergent residues.

🔍
1. Inspection & Spotting
Fiber identification (Wool, Nylon, Polyester, Acrylic)
High-power industrial pre-vacuuming
Targeted stain pre-spray treatment (wine, coffee, blood)
Eco-friendly carpet sanitising traffic lane spray
💨
2. Hot Water Extraction
Fiber agitation to loosen deep-seated dirt and soil
Hot water extraction at 110°C to sanitise and rinse
Dual-motor high vacuum lift to recover 93% of water
Residue-free clean (no crunchy post-cleaning feel)
🍃
3. Deodorising & Sanitising
Aroma infusion (eliminates pet and musty odours)
Allergen and dust mite extraction
pH balance rinse to lock in carpet softness
Bacteria-killing sanitisation agent application

4. Pile Grooming & Care
Carpet rake grooming to align pile fibers
Special protection spray application (optional add-on)
Drying airflow setup recommendations
Post-clean inspection with client satisfaction sign-off

When carpet cleaning isn't enough

Steam cleaning handles heavy traffic, stains, and sanitisation. However, it cannot solve structural issues:

Permanent chemical burns (bleach, severe dye transfer)
Severe sub-floor pet urine saturation (requires carpet replacement)
Extremely worn or threadbare areas (fibers are physically gone)
Major flood extraction (refer to emergency water restoration)

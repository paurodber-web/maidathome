## Why End Of Lease Cleaning Costs More Than A  Regular Clean

A standard fortnightly or weekly clean keeps
a home maintained. An end of lease clean
is a completely different scope of work.
Cleaners are required to bring every
part of the property to a near-showroom
standard. That means inside ovens,
inside cupboards, exhaust fans, window
tracks, laundry areas and everything in
between. This level of detail takes
significantly more time and expertise
than a regular visit.

Real estate agents perform detailed
inspections using checklists, and
anything missed can result in a failed
inspection and a bond deduction. A
proper bond clean is essentially an
insurance policy for your bond.

## What Affects The Final Price

The prices above give you a solid benchmark,
but a few things can push the cost
higher depending on your specific
property.

### Property Condition

A property that has been kept on top of
throughout the tenancy will take less
time to clean at the end. One that
hasn't had a professional service in
months, or where grease and grime has
built up over time, will take more
effort to bring up to inspection
standard.

### Number Of Bathrooms

Properties with two or more bathrooms take
considerably longer to clean compared to
single-bathroom homes of the same
bedroom count. If your 3-bedroom home
has two full bathrooms, expect the price
to sit toward the higher end of the
range.

### Lease Agreement Requirements

Some leases specify that certain services,
such as professional carpet steam
cleaning or blind cleaning, must be
carried out by a licensed technician.
Always review your lease agreement
before booking so you can include all
required add-ons in a single visit.

### Property Size And Layout

Open-plan homes with large living and dining
areas often take longer than a compact
apartment with the same number of
bedrooms. Total floor area and the
number of rooms will always play a role
in the final time and cost.

## DIY Vs. Professional End Of Lease Cleaning:  Is It Worth It?

Many tenants try to clean the property
themselves to save money. In practice,

### The Risk Of A Failed Inspection

Real estate agents use detailed checklists

inside exhaust fans, inside oven
cavities, skirting boards, window
tracks, and the underside of range
hoods. A failed inspection means you'll
need to arrange another clean anyway,
and you risk losing hundreds of dollars
from your bond in the meantime.

A thorough end of lease clean on a 2-bedroom

equipment. Most tenants are also
managing the stress of moving during
this time, which makes a rushed or
incomplete clean even more costly. A
professional team completes the job
efficiently, with the right tools and
products, and stands behind their work.

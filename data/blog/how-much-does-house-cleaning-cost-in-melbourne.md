# House Cleaning Costs In Melbourne: Honest Prices By Home Size And Service Type

If you are comparing cleaning services in Melbourne, the real question is not just what a cleaner charges. It is what that price includes, whether it is fixed or hourly, and whether you are looking at a regular [standard clean](https://maidathome.com.au/services/standard-cleaning-melbourne/), a one-off [deep clean](https://maidathome.com.au/services/deep-cleaning-melbourne/), or an [end of lease clean](https://maidathome.com.au/services/end-of-lease-cleaning-melbourne/).

## Quick Answer: How Much Does House Cleaning Cost In Melbourne?

For many Melbourne homes, standard house cleaning starts from around $130 to $170 for smaller properties and often lands in the $180 to $260+ range for larger 3 to 4 bedroom homes. Deep cleaning and end of lease cleaning usually cost more because they involve heavier detail work, more time, and a broader scope.

The fastest way to interpret pricing is this: routine recurring cleaning is usually the lowest-cost option, while one-off deep cleaning and move-out cleaning sit higher because the cleaners are doing more catch-up or inspection-level work.

| Service Type | Typical Pricing Style | Estimated Range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hourly cleaning | Per cleaner / per hour | Often around $45 to $65+ per hour |
| 1 bedroom or studio | Fixed price | From $130 to $170 |
| 2 bedroom home | Fixed price | Around $150 to $210 |
| 3 to 4 bedroom home | Fixed price | Around $180 to $260+ |

These are guide ranges, not one-size-fits-all promises. Exact pricing still depends on service type, home condition, number of bathrooms, and any extras such as oven cleaning, inside windows, balcony cleaning, or steam cleaning.

## Average Hourly Cleaner Rates In Melbourne

Hourly rates in Melbourne vary widely because they do not always describe the same thing. Some companies quote per cleaner, some per team, and some advertise a low rate before travel, supplies, or minimum booking windows are factored in.

In practical terms, many homeowners find flat pricing easier to compare because the total is clearer upfront. Hourly pricing can still make sense for smaller, flexible jobs, but it often becomes harder to compare fairly when two providers include different tasks in the same number of hours.

## Standard Cleaning Prices By Home Size

Most Melbourne households looking for ongoing cleaning want a realistic range by property size, not a vague promise. That is why fixed-price packages are common for routine home cleaning.

### 1 Bedroom Or Studio Apartment

Smaller homes are usually the most affordable because there is less floor area, fewer surfaces, and often only one bathroom. For regular maintenance cleaning, this is where you commonly see guide prices starting from around $130 to $170.

### 2 Bedroom Home

This is one of the most common price points in Melbourne. A 2-bedroom home often moves into the mid-range because there is usually more furniture, a second sleeping area, and sometimes an extra bathroom or powder room. If the property is cleaned weekly or fortnightly, pricing tends to stay more stable.

### 3 To 4 Bedroom House

Once you move into family-home territory, time increases quickly. More bedrooms usually means more floors, more bathrooms, more dusting, and more lived-in surfaces. That is why many larger homes in suburbs such as Brighton, Kew, or Toorak tend to sit in the $180 to $260+ range for standard maintenance cleaning.

## Deep Cleaning Prices By Home Size

Deep cleaning sits above routine house cleaning because the scope is more detailed. Cleaners are usually dealing with built-up grime, skirting boards, extra bathroom detail, kitchen degreasing, and the sort of reset work that goes beyond maintenance.

For smaller homes, deep cleaning often starts above standard-clean pricing and rises quickly as bathrooms, condition, and add-ons increase. If the property has not been professionally cleaned for a while, a [deep clean](https://maidathome.com.au/services/deep-cleaning-melbourne/) is often the right first booking before switching to recurring maintenance.

## End Of Lease Cleaning Prices By Home Size

End of lease pricing is usually the highest of the three major service types because the work is broader, the standard is stricter, and many properties also need extras such as carpet steam cleaning, oven cleaning, inside windows, or balcony cleaning.

Smaller apartments may start from the lower end of the range, while 2 and 3 bedroom properties move up quickly depending on bathrooms, condition, and whether the property manager expects a more detailed finish. ## Flat Rate Vs Hourly Cleaning: Which Works Out Better?

Both pricing models exist for a reason. The better option depends on whether you want flexibility or certainty.

### When Hourly Cleaning Makes Sense

Hourly pricing can work when the scope is unclear, when you only want help with selected rooms, or when you want a cleaner to work through a priority list rather than complete a full-home service.

### When Flat Rate Cleaning Is Usually Better

For most regular home cleaning, fixed pricing is easier to budget for and easier to compare. You know the likely total before the booking starts, which matters if you are choosing between a standard clean, a first-time reset, or a recurring service.

### Why Home Size Changes The Answer

The larger and more predictable the property, the more useful flat pricing becomes. A family home in Hawthorn or Camberwell is usually easier to price fairly by scope than by an open-ended hourly estimate.

## What Affects The Price Of A House Clean In Melbourne?

Two homes can have the same number of bedrooms and still receive very different quotes. That is because pricing is shaped by more than just floorplan.

### Frequency Of Cleaning

Weekly and fortnightly cleans are generally better value than monthly or one-off visits. When a cleaner is maintaining a home consistently, there is less buildup to deal with each time. That makes the visit more efficient and usually reduces cost per appointment.

### Condition Of The Property

This is one of the biggest pricing variables. A tidy, well-kept home is faster to clean than a home that has not had attention in weeks or months. Kitchens with grease buildup, bathrooms with soap scum, and dusty skirting or blinds all increase the time required.

### Bathrooms, Kitchens, And Detail Work

Bathrooms and kitchens are usually the most time-intensive zones in a regular clean. A home with more wet areas or a kitchen that needs more detail will naturally cost more than a home with the same bedroom count but fewer high-effort spaces.

### Location Within Melbourne

Travel, parking, access, and service area logistics can all affect pricing at the margins. A compact apartment in South Yarra with tricky lift access may be very different from an easy-access family home in Brighton or Kew, even if the bedroom count looks similar on paper.

### Optional Extras

Oven cleaning, inside windows, balcony wash-downs, carpet steam cleaning, and similar add-ons are usually not part of a standard regular clean. If you need them, they should be priced separately so you can compare like for like.

## FAQs

### How Much Does A Cleaner Cost Per Hour In Melbourne?

Hourly rates vary by provider, team structure, and scope, but many Melbourne households see figures around the mid-range of the local market rather than a single universal price. For regular house cleaning, fixed pricing is usually easier to compare.

### Is Weekly Cleaning Cheaper Than A One-Off Clean?

Usually yes. Weekly or fortnightly cleaning tends to be better value because the home stays maintained and requires less catch-up work each visit.

### What Is Usually Included In A Standard House Clean?

A standard clean typically includes dusting, vacuuming, mopping, bathroom cleaning, and kitchen surface wiping. Exact inclusions vary, which is why it is important to compare scope as well as price.

### When Should I Book A Deep Clean Instead?

If the home has not been professionally cleaned for a while, or if it needs detail work beyond routine maintenance, a deep clean is often the better starting point. It resets the property to a higher standard before ongoing regular visits.
